Why Tigard Homeowners Need a Dedicated Removal Specialist

Most roofing contractors in Washington County will tell you they can handle the solar panels as part of your re-roof. What they won't tell you is that Oregon law requires solar removal and reinstallation to be performed by a contractor holding a valid Oregon CCB license and a state electrical license. A roofing crew that moves your panels without the right credentials puts your manufacturer warranty at risk and creates potential liability if something goes wrong with the electrical system.

We work exclusively on solar removal and reinstallation. That focus means faster turnarounds, more careful handling, and full accountability for every panel we touch. Frequently Asked Questions — Tigard Solar Removal

My roofer said they remove solar panels all the time. Should I let them?

Ask them for their Oregon CCB number and their Oregon electrical license before agreeing. Removing a solar system requires licensed electrical work in Oregon — it's not just unbolting panels. If your roofer can't provide both credentials, the work isn't legally or safely within their scope. Unlicensed removal also risks voiding your panel and inverter warranties.

What if our Tigard home has an older string inverter system?

Older string inverter systems — common on systems installed before 2015 — require additional care during disconnection because they operate at higher DC voltages than newer microinverter systems. Our licensed electricians are trained on both system types. We'll note the inverter type during our initial assessment and adjust our disconnection procedure accordingly.

We do our best. During peak season, July through September, we're often booked two to three weeks out. If your roofer is already scheduled and you haven't arranged solar removal yet, call us immediately — we'll tell you honestly whether the timeline is workable and what options exist if it isn't.
