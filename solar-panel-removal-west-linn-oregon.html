Why West Linn Homeowners Need a Dedicated Removal Specialist

Most roofing contractors in Clackamas County will tell you they can handle the solar panels as part of your re-roof. What they won't tell you is that Oregon law requires solar removal and reinstallation to be performed by a contractor holding a valid Oregon CCB license and a state electrical license. A roofing crew that moves your panels without the right credentials puts your manufacturer warranty at risk and creates potential liability if something goes wrong with the electrical system.

We work exclusively on solar removal and reinstallation. That focus means faster turnarounds, more careful handling, and full accountability for every panel we touch. PGE and Clackamas County — What West Linn Homeowners Need to Know

West Linn is in PGE territory. Your net metering agreement stays active during a temporary removal — no reapplication needed. PGE must be notified before de-energization, which we handle as a standard step. Clackamas County permits for removal typically take two to four weeks to process. With a roofing season that runs mid-July through mid-September, start the solar removal process no later than early June.

What to Expect on a West Linn Solar Removal Job

  1. Pre-Job System Benchmark

    We document your system's current output before anything is touched — giving us a verified baseline to confirm after reinstallation.

  2. PGE Utility Notification

    We notify PGE before de-energizing your system so your net metering interconnection stays intact throughout the project.

  3. Licensed Electrical Disconnection

    Solar panels generate electricity even on overcast Pacific Northwest days. Our licensed electricians shut the system down safely at the inverter before any panels are touched.

  4. Panel Removal, Labeling & Storage

    Every panel is labeled, photographed, and stored securely on your property. Racking and flashing are removed cleanly so your roofer has a clear deck to work from.

  5. Reinstallation to Manufacturer Specs

    Once your roofer is done, we return and reinstall to original specifications — preserving your panel and inverter warranties.

  6. Post-Reinstall Performance Verification

    We confirm your system hits its pre-removal output before we close the job. You receive a written performance report.
