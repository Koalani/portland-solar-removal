PGE in Sherwood — Net Metering During Removal

Sherwood is in PGE's service territory. Your net metering interconnection agreement stays active during a temporary removal for roof work — no reapplication required. We notify PGE before your system is de-energized, handle all utility coordination, and confirm your system is back to full production before closing the job. You don't need to contact PGE at any point in the process.

Frequently Asked Questions — Sherwood Solar Removal

We're planning to upgrade our panels at the same time as the re-roof. Does that change anything?

If you're upgrading to new panels, the removal process is the same — we take the existing panels down and store them. The reinstallation is where things differ: new panels may use different mounting hardware and require a new permit for the installation phase, which your new solar installer handles. We coordinate the removal so the deck is ready for whoever is doing the new install.

How do we handle the re-roof coordination if we haven't booked a roofer yet?

Get your solar removal quote first and confirm our availability. Then book your roofer around our schedule. We can provide you with a removal completion date that your roofer can use as their start date — that simple handoff is what keeps the project on track without gaps or overlap between crews.

Is Sherwood subject to HOA approval for solar panel removal?

HOA rules vary by development. Most HOAs in Sherwood don't require approval for temporary removal during roof replacement — it's considered maintenance, not a new installation. However, we recommend checking with your HOA before scheduling, particularly if your system is visible from the street. Reinstallation to the original configuration typically doesn't require new HOA approval.
