Why Tualatin Homeowners Need a Dedicated Removal Specialist

Most roofing contractors in Washington County will tell you they can handle the solar panels as part of your re-roof. What they won't tell you is that Oregon law requires solar removal and reinstallation to be performed by a contractor holding a valid Oregon CCB license and a state electrical license. A roofing crew that moves your panels without the right credentials puts your manufacturer warranty at risk and creates potential liability if something goes wrong with the electrical system.

We work exclusively on solar removal and reinstallation. That focus means faster turnarounds, more careful handling, and full accountability for every panel we touch. Before hiring anyone, ask directly: "What is your Oregon CCB number and can you show me your electrical license?" Any legitimate contractor answers without hesitation.

Washington County Permit — Start Early

Tualatin solar removal requires a Washington County electrical permit. Processing takes two to four weeks. The dry roofing window in the Portland metro runs mid-July through mid-September. That means if you want your roof done before fall rains arrive, you need solar removal booked by late May or June at the latest. We handle permit submission as part of every job.

PGE Net Metering — What Stays Active During Removal

Tualatin is PGE territory. Your net metering interconnection agreement remains active during a temporary removal for roof work — you do not need to reapply or lose your net metering status. PGE must be notified before your system is de-energized, which we handle as a standard step. Billing pauses during downtime and resumes once production is confirmed after reinstallation.

Frequently Asked Questions — Tualatin Solar Removal

Is there any risk of damage to the panels during storage while the roofer works?

Not when storage is done correctly. Every panel is labeled, photographed, and stored flat on your property — never stacked at stress-inducing angles. We conduct a visual inspection of each panel during removal and document any pre-existing damage in writing before we start. That written record protects both you and us if any questions arise later.

How do I know the system will work the same after reinstallation?

We benchmark your system's output before removal and run a post-reinstall performance test before we close the job. If output doesn't match the pre-removal baseline, we diagnose and resolve it before leaving. You receive a written performance report documenting both readings. That's your warranty that the reinstall was done correctly.

Can we schedule removal and reinstallation in the same week as the roofing crew?

Removal and roofing can happen in the same week — removal on day one, roofing on days two through four, reinstallation on day five is a common sequence for straightforward jobs. The key is confirming your roofer's timeline in advance. We schedule directly around your roofing crew's start and finish dates to minimize total system downtime.
