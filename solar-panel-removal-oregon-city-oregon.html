Why Oregon City Homeowners Need a Specialist — Not Their Roofer

Most roofing contractors in Clackamas County will tell you they can handle your solar panels. What they won't tell you is that touching your system without a valid Oregon electrical license can void your panel manufacturer warranty and expose you to liability if something goes wrong on the roof or in your electrical system.

Oregon requires solar removal and reinstallation to be performed by a contractor holding both an Oregon Construction Contractors Board (CCB) license and the appropriate electrical credential. Before you hire anyone, ask directly: "Can I see your CCB number and your electrical license?" If they hesitate, walk away.

At Portland Solar Removal & Reinstall Pros, we carry both. We work exclusively on removal and reinstallation — it's all we do, which means we're faster, more careful, and more accountable than a roofing crew treating your array as an afterthought.

What to Expect During the Removal & Reinstall Process

We follow a structured, documented process on every Oregon City job. Nothing is improvised, and every panel is labeled and logged before it comes off the roof.

  1. Pre-Job System Diagnostic

    We benchmark your system's current output before anything is touched — so we have a baseline to verify against after reinstallation.

  2. Utility Notification (Pacific Power / PGE)

    Oregon City is served by Pacific Power. We handle all required utility notification so your net metering interconnection agreement stays intact during the downtime. You don't need to call anyone.

  3. Safe Electrical Disconnection

    Every panel generates electricity in daylight — even overcast Pacific Northwest days. Our licensed electricians shut down the system safely at the inverter and disconnect properly before any physical removal begins.

  4. Panel Removal, Labeling & On-Site Storage

    Each panel is labeled, documented, and stored securely on your property. Racking and flashing are carefully removed to leave your roofer a clean deck.

  5. Reinstallation to Original Specs

    Once your roofer is done, we return and reinstall to manufacturer specifications — preserving your panel warranty and your inverter warranty.

  6. Post-Reinstall Performance Test

    We verify your system hits its pre-removal output before we close out the job. You get a written performance report.

What Does Solar Panel Removal Cost in Oregon City?

For a typical Oregon City residential system of 15–25 panels, removal and reinstallation runs $4,000–$8,000 depending on system size, roof pitch, and mounting hardware condition. Older systems with weathered hardware take more time and run toward the higher end.

A few things that affect your specific cost: steep Clackamas County hillside roofs require additional fall protection equipment; systems older than 10 years often have stiffened mounting hardware that takes more time to remove safely; and partial removal (for a section repair rather than full re-roof) carries a minimum service charge regardless of panel count.

Frequently Asked Questions — Oregon City Solar Removal

Does my net metering agreement with Pacific Power get affected when panels are removed?

Your interconnection agreement stays in place during a temporary removal for roof work — it does not need to be reapplied for. However, Pacific Power does need to be notified before your system is de-energized. We handle that notification as part of every job so there's no lapse in your agreement and no confusion about your billing status during the downtime.

Do I need a permit to remove and reinstall solar panels in Oregon City?

Yes. Clackamas County requires permits for solar removal and reinstallation whenever electrical work is involved — which it always is. Permit timelines in Oregon typically run one to four weeks. We pull the permit as part of our process, and we factor that timeline into the project schedule so your roofer isn't waiting on paperwork.

How far in advance should I book before my roof replacement?

During peak roofing season in Oregon City — July through September — we recommend booking your solar removal at least three to four weeks ahead of your roofing start date. This accounts for permitting time and ensures our crew and your roofer can be properly sequenced. Booking after your roofer is already scheduled often means delays for everyone.
