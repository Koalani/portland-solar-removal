Why Beaverton Homeowners Need a Dedicated Removal Specialist

Beaverton has one of the highest solar adoption rates in Washington County. That means there are also a lot of Beaverton homeowners who are now hitting the 10–15 year mark on their roofs — and discovering that re-roofing with solar panels installed is more complicated than they expected.

Most roofing contractors will tell you they can handle the panels. What they won't mention is that Oregon law requires solar removal and reinstallation to be performed by a contractor holding a valid Oregon CCB license and an Oregon electrical license. A roofing crew that moves your panels without the right credentials puts your manufacturer warranty at risk and creates potential liability if something goes wrong electrically.

We work exclusively on removal and reinstallation — nothing else. That focus means we're faster, more careful, and fully accountable for every panel we touch. Before hiring anyone, ask for their CCB number and their electrical license. If they can't produce both, don't hire them.

Washington County Permit Reality

Solar removal and reinstallation in Beaverton requires a Washington County electrical permit. Processing currently runs two to four weeks. If you're planning a summer roof replacement — Beaverton's dry roofing window runs mid-July through mid-September — you need to start the solar removal process by late May or early June at the latest. Waiting until your roofer is already booked is how projects get delayed.

What Happens to Your PGE Connection During Removal

Beaverton sits in PGE territory. When your panels come down, your system is de-energized — which raises a question most homeowners have but few contractors answer clearly: what happens to your net metering agreement?

The short answer is your interconnection agreement stays active. A temporary removal for roof work does not require you to reapply. However, PGE must be notified before your system is de-energized. We handle that notification as a standard part of every job. Your net metering billing pauses during downtime and resumes automatically once the system is back online and producing.

You do not need to call PGE. You do not need to file anything. We take care of it.

Frequently Asked Questions — Beaverton Solar Removal

My solar company says they can do the removal when I need a re-roof. Should I just use them?

Possibly — but verify their credentials first. Ask specifically for their Oregon CCB number and their Oregon electrical license. Many solar installation companies are licensed for new installs but don't carry the right credentials for removal and reinstallation as a standalone service. If they can't provide both licenses immediately, get a second opinion before proceeding. Using an unlicensed contractor for removal can void your panel manufacturer warranty.

How far in advance should I book solar removal before my Beaverton roof replacement?

During peak season — mid-July through mid-September — book at least three to four weeks ahead of your roofing start date. This accounts for Washington County permit processing time and gives both crews room to sequence properly. If your roofer is already scheduled and you haven't lined up solar removal yet, call us immediately. We'll tell you honestly whether the timeline is workable.

Will my solar panels be damaged during removal and storage?

Not when the job is done correctly. Every panel is individually labeled, photographed, and stored flat on your property — never stacked at angles that stress the glass or frames. We conduct a visual inspection of each panel during removal and flag any pre-existing damage in writing before we start, so there's never a dispute about the condition of your equipment.
